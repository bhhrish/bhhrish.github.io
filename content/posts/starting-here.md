Five years of reading eventually demands an outlet.

I started with the Ramayana, moved into the Mahabharata, and somewhere along the way found myself pulling threads from the Upanishads, the Bhagavad Gita, and various commentaries. The more I read, the more I noticed how often the popular understanding of these texts differs from what the texts actually say — or at least from what I found when I sat with them carefully.

This blog exists to work through those differences out loud.

A few things to set expectations:

**I am not an authority.** I have no formal training in Sanskrit or Indology. I read translations, multiple versions where possible, and secondary scholarship. My interpretations are my own and could be wrong.

**I welcome disagreement.** If you have read something that contradicts what I write here, say so. The best outcome of any post is a conversation.

**The focus is the two Itihasas.** The Ramayana and the Mahabharata are where I have spent most of my time and where I feel I have something worth saying. Other texts will appear where they are relevant.

That is enough preamble. The posts will speak for themselves.
